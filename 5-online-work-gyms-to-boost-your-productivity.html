5 Online Work Gyms to Boost Your Productivity

The evolving remote work model makes staying focused and productive more challenging. Enter online work gyms: dedicated virtual spaces that offer a unique blend of structured work sessions, accountability, and a sense of community.

Online work gyms are ideally suited for individuals who thrive in deep work sessions and desire a supportive environment to overcome the isolation associated with remote work. Here are some online work gyms you can try today.

1. Caveday

Caveday stands out as a pioneering online work gym, redefining the concept of productivity. At its core, it uses structured work sessions called “Caves,” during which participants commit to focused work for a designated period.

There’s a dedicated schedule for each of these “Caves” which is updated on the website periodically. Within virtual rooms, individuals can set goals and hold each other accountable, creating an atmosphere of collective productivity.

2. Focusmate

If external accountability boosts your productivity, Focusmate might be your perfect fit. Focusmate’s sessions connect you with a virtual accountability partner for work sessions. By mimicking the experience of a physical co-working space, Focusmate allows you to see and hear your partner while you work.

The sessions can be 20, 50, or 75 minutes long, during which you can work on any task. The added presence also helps minimize distractions and fosters an added sense of accountability which can drive you to accomplish more.

3. Bramble

Bramble offers a unique online work gym experience by capturing the spontaneity and energy of physical co-working spaces. With its interactive virtual rooms that use “fluid video tech,” you can create your own avatar, customize and create the rooms to your liking.

Bramble allows you to collaborate with colleagues, join impromptu discussions, and create private spaces for focused work. The platform's customizable features provide flexibility, giving you the power to tailor your environment to your specific work needs.

4. Flown

For individuals seeking a structured approach to work and intentional breaks, Flown is an ideal online work gym. The platform leans heavily on incorporating deep work into your routine and integrates the Pomodoro technique, dividing work into focused sprints called “Flocks” followed by timed breaks led by expert facilitators. The different Flocks include:

  • Deep Dives: 2-hour sessions with a small group.
  • Power Hour: 1-hour sessions.
  • Take-Off: 20-minute routines for journaling and meditation.
  • Drop-In: All-day silent work room.

5. Groove

Recognizing that productivity and camaraderie go hand in hand, Groove takes a holistic approach to online work gyms. This platform combines work sessions with deeper collaboration and friendship, allowing participants to support each other and get familiar.

Groove incorporates 50-minute sessions during which you're placed in a virtual room with one to three other Groovers. With your mic off, the Groovers participating can cheer you on (and you, them) through the written chat option as you get things done.

The platform encourages camaraderie by helping you find your own “Groove crew” to vibe with. Groove is free to use but with a catch. It’s an exclusive community, so you have to fill out an application to join. If it gets vetted, you’re in for a ‘groovy’ experience!

Give Your Productivity a Workout

Whether you prefer structured work sessions like those offered by Caveday, collaborative environments like Bramble, or a holistic approach like Flown, there is an online work gym that aligns with your needs.

By embracing these innovative platforms, you can boost your productivity, combat isolation, and unlock your full professional potential in the virtual workspace.
