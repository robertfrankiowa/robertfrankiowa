How long does the teeth straightening process take with aligners?

Expert opinion from Dr. KODALI RAGA SRI SAI MALIKA
Dentist · 2.6 · India

Firstly, aligners are used for correction of malaligned teeth. The treatment with aligners might take 6 months to 2 years or even longer. The period of correction for straightening the teeth depends on one's individual level of malalignment. For example, if malaignment is less, only involving one or two tooth, it might be corrected earlier, within 6 months to 1 year, but if malalignment is severe with more crowding, then it might take more than a year or two.

Expert opinion from Dr. Shalini Verma
Bachelor of Dental Surgery · Less than a year of experience · India

For alignment of teeth with aligners, it takes around 6 to 18 months of time for the expected results to be achieved. Aligners are clear braces for straightening of teeth. Unlike, metal braces they take lesser time.

What are the benefits of using teeth straightening aligners over traditional braces?

Expert opinion from Dr. KODALI RAGA SRI SAI MALIKA
Dentist · 2.6 · India

Aligners are used for correcting malaligned teeth. Advantages of aligners over braces are, aligners are invisible, they are more esthetic, so patient concern about esthetics is not compromised with aligners. They are more convenient to wear than braces, where in case traditional braces, impingement to lips and gums are more common. Proper oral hygiene and cleaning of the gums are maintained with aligners, so that less prone to chronic gum diseases, they are easily removed and no food restrictions.

Expert opinion from Dr. SAMEEHA M S
Four Years of Clinical Experience, BDS - Kerala, India · 1 years of experience · India

When compared to traditional braces, teeth straightening aligners, such as clear aligners, provide various advantages. The possible advantages of teeth straightening aligners over conventional braces include aesthetic appeal, removability, comfort, convenience, fewer dental appointments, better oral hygiene, and a lower risk of tooth decay and gum problems throughout the treatment. It is essential to note that, while aligners are a great option for many people, they may not be appropriate for difficult orthodontic cases. Traditional braces or alternative orthodontic procedures may be more suited in such circumstances.

Are there any risks or side effects associated with using teeth straightening aligners?

Expert opinion from Dr. KODALI RAGA SRI SAI MALIKA
Dentist · 2.6 · India

Side effects using teeth straightening aligners are painful chewing, lip and cheek biting, allergic reactions, some will be having issues with the fit of the aligners. Other side effects include dry mouth, bad breath, lisping that may cause speech difficulty, and bite issues.
