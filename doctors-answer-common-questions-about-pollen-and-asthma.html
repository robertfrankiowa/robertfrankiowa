How does pollen trigger asthma symptoms and attacks?

Expert opinion from Dr. Anuvitha Kamath
MBBS · 3 years of experience · India

In asthmatic people, pollen exposure can lead to the triggering and worsening of symptoms. Pollen-induced asthma is when there is a narrowing of the airways due to pollen exposure. The symptoms of this are shortness of breath, difficulty breathing, wheezing, and coughing. This condition is termed pollen-induced bronchoconstriction. This is due to the release of a chemical called histamine which leads to excess mucus production and thus blocks and narrows the air passages, making it difficult to breathe and causing asthma.

Expert opinion from Dr. Himabindu Sreenivasulu
MBBS · 1 years of experience · India

Pollen can trigger asthma symptoms and attacks in individuals who are allergic to it. When a person with asthma inhales pollen, their immune system recognizes it as a threat and overreacts, leading to an inflammatory response in the airways. This immune response causes the airways to become swollen, and narrow, and produce excessive mucus, making it difficult to breathe. Common asthma symptoms triggered by pollen include wheezing, coughing, shortness of breath, and chest tightness. The severity of the symptoms can vary depending on the individual's sensitivity to pollen and the amount of exposure. It's important for individuals with pollen-triggered asthma to take appropriate preventive measures, such as avoiding pollen exposure, following their prescribed asthma management plan, and using prescribed medications to keep symptoms under control.

Expert opinion from Dr. Himabindu Sreenivasulu
MBBS · 1 years of experience · India

Pollen exposure can pose significant risks and complications for people with asthma and other respiratory conditions. When individuals with asthma inhale pollen, it can trigger asthma symptoms and exacerbate breathing difficulties. Pollen can cause the airways to become inflamed and narrow, leading to wheezing, coughing, shortness of breath, and chest tightness. In severe cases, it can result in asthma attacks, which can be life-threatening. For individuals with other respiratory conditions, such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) or bronchitis, pollen exposure can worsen respiratory symptoms and decrease lung function. It's crucial for individuals with asthma and respiratory conditions to take preventive measures, such as staying indoors during high pollen times, using air purifiers, taking prescribed medications as directed, and following an asthma action plan provided by their healthcare professional. Seeking immediate medical attention in case of severe symptoms is also essential to manage the risks associated with pollen exposure for those with respiratory conditions.

What are the best ways to prevent and treat pollen allergies and asthma?

Expert opinion from Dr. Anuvitha Kamath
MBBS · 3 years of experience · India

In asthmatic people, pollen exposure leads to the narrowing of the airways due to the release of histamine which causes excess mucus production and blocks the airways. The symptoms of this are shortness of breath, difficulty breathing, wheezing, and coughing. This condition is termed pollen-induced bronchoconstriction. The best way to reduce allergies is to avoid all the triggering factors. Staying indoors, and wearing a mask while going out, using allergen-proof bed covers, changing bedding weekly, maintaining low humidity, using clean carpets have been very effective in treating allergies due to pollen. Antihistamine drugs must be consumed to reduce allergic reactions. In the case of nasal stuffiness caused due to allergies, nasal decongestants can be used. In severe cases of allergy, steroids also may be given.
