Traveling Can Make You Happier Than Getting Married, According to a Study

Whether traveling light with just a backpack or indulging in the luxuries of a five-star hotel, all travel experiences have a profound impact on one's health. One of these benefits of traveling is delivering a significant boost in endorphins. For some, the joy derived from travel can surpass that of life's milestone events, such as marriage or the birth of a child. A study conducted by Booking.com reveals that travel ranks as the highest source of happiness for many people.

Immediate Happiness Boost from The Benefits of Traveling

To explore this phenomenon, Booking.com surveyed 17,000 people from 17 different countries to gather a wide range of perspectives on travel. The results were striking: a significant majority indicated that few things can match the joy of going on vacation. Specifically, 77% of respondents reported booking a holiday to instantly improve their mood.

Travel Versus Major Life Events

One of the most surprising findings of the study was the extent to which people value travel over other significant life events. Nearly half (49%) of those surveyed said planning and embarking on a vacation brought them more happiness than their wedding day. Surely one of the most unassuming benefits of traveling.

Additionally, almost half of the respondents found that dating did not boost their spirits as much as travel did. With 45% stating the same about getting engaged. Even the arrival of a new baby did not compare for 29% of the respondents when it came to the endorphin rush provided by travel.

Experiences Over Material Possessions – Benefits of Traveling

The preference for experiences over material goods was also evident. Seventy percent of participants indicated that the joy from a vacation outlasted the happiness from purchasing new items. This inclination toward experiences was further highlighted by the fact that 56% would choose travel over buying new clothes or gadgets. While 48% were willing to delay home improvements to explore new destinations.

The Joy of Planning a Vacation

Interestingly, the anticipation of a trip alone can enhance emotional well-being. For 72% of respondents, merely planning a vacation was enough to boost their happiness. A substantial 80% felt a surge of excitement just by looking at maps and picking potential travel destinations.

Reading hotel reviews and imagining future trips provided an emotional lift for 60% of those surveyed. However, shopping for vacation attire was cited by more than half as one of the greatest mood enhancers. Which is a top benefit of traveling for many.

Conclusion

This comprehensive study by Booking.com underscores the profound impact of travel on personal happiness and well-being. From the planning stages to the actual journey, the joy derived from exploring new places and cultures is unmatched by material possessions or even some of life's most significant events. The findings suggest a growing trend toward valuing experiences over things. Reflecting a deeper understanding of what truly brings joy and fulfillment.
