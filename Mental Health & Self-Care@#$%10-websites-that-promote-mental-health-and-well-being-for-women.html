10 Websites That Promote Mental Health and Well-Being for Women

  • MGH Center for Women's Mental Health offers informative blogs, research-backed resources, and educational programs on women's mental health and well-being.
  • Ashley Comegys provides online counseling for women dealing with anxiety, postpartum conditions, and more, along with a blog dedicated to women's emotional and mental health.
  • Therapy for Black Girls is an online platform for Black women that offers mental health information, professional help, an online community, and a podcast discussing various topics.

Anxiety, depression, and PTSD are just a handful of common mental health conditions that women can experience. If you're a woman and you've faced any of these conditions, you might be wondering what you can do to look after yourself and your mental health.

Read on to find out more about the websites and online resources that are available to promote mental health and well-being for women.

1. MGH Center for Women's Mental Health

The Massachusetts General Hospital Center for Women's Mental Health focuses on women's mental health and how it changes across their lifespan as well as ways to improve our mental health backed by research.

Additionally, there's a range of educational programs—such as live online courses and on-demand courses covering topics like perinatal psychiatry and psychiatric disorders in Women.

2. Ashley Comegys

If you think your mental health would benefit from therapy, but you don't have the time or money—or you simply can't find a reliable therapist—Ashley Comegys could be the answer for you.

Ashley specializes in online counseling and therapy for women dealing with anxiety, postpartum conditions, grief and loss, trauma, and depression. In addition, she also offers support for military spouses and mothers with anxiety.

Besides online therapy, the Ashley Comegys website is also home to Exhale. Exhale is a blog dedicated to women's emotional and mental health.

3. Therapy for Black Girls

Therapy for Black Girls is an online platform where Black women can access relevant mental health information and professional help.

It is truly an extensive online space with everything from an online blog and Therapy for Black Girls University to the online community and chat called Sister Circle.

Furthermore, be sure to listen to the Therapy for Black Girls podcast with Dr. Joy Harden Bradford, where she discusses topics such as impostor syndrome, maintaining friendships, and demystifying menopause.

4. Sanity Daily

Founded by Priyanka Joshi, a mental health blogger, Sanity Daily is an online space that centers on helping people better their mental health and well-being. Sanity Daily features both a blog and a podcast with handy mental health tips, insights, and strategies.

What makes the Sanity Daily blog particularly beneficial for women is that it covers topics like health and wellness for single moms, domestic violence's impact on women's mental health, and self-care ideas for women.

Alternatively, the Sanity Daily podcast is also worth listening to, as some of the episodes talk about self-confidence, overthinking, and setting boundaries.

5. The Bipolar Buzz

Even though both men and women can have bipolar disorder, depressive symptoms and related conditions are more common in women—as stated in this study from the Indian Journal of Psychiatry.

Lai and D, both diagnosed with bipolar, created the website The Bipolar Buzz to help people better understand and deal with the illness.

The online platform offers something for everyone, whether you want to read The Bipolar Buzz blog, listen to the podcast, or join the DARE Academy. The DARE Academy is an online course that you can sign up for if you need a little help to overcome daily feelings of anxiety, fear, and panic.

6. Anxious Lass

If you're a woman who is interested in a website dedicated to social anxiety, Anxious Lass is the online platform for you. Anxious Lass features an online community, a mental health blog, an e-book, and a plethora of online resources.

Looking for daily tips straight to your inbox? You can even sign up for the Anxious Lass's free 7-Day Anxiety Course.

7. Healthline

You might know Healthline as one of the best online platforms to find health guidance, medical information, and wellness advice. That being said, Healthline actually has an entire section for women's wellness.

Here, you'll find everything you need as a woman—from career-crushing tips and tricks and practicing mindfulness stress reduction, to information on how couples can improve their relationships.

What's more, Healthline even features women's health product reviews for products that are related to sexual health and wellness, menstrual health, and fertility and pregnancy.

8. Natasha Tracy

Natasha Tracy's online platform is home to her renowned mental health blog, Bipolar Burble. But apart from her blog, she offers so much more, including various mental health masterclasses, free mini webinars, and tons of handy mental health resources for specific disorders like bipolar, depression, and more.

Moreover, there's the podcast, Snap Out of It! that centers on mental health in the workplace. Feel free to listen to it on your favorite streaming platform, be it Apple Podcasts, Google Podcasts, or Spotify.

9. Alison Dotson

According to an article from Current Psychiatry Reports, obsessive compulsive disorder (OCD) is more common in adolescent and adult women.

Alison Dotson's website is where she shares her experiences with OCD after being diagnosed herself and offers OCD information and support through her blog. Moreover, her blog features weekly Q&A sessions with various OCD advocates and special guests like Rev. Katie O'Dunne, Peter Cannon, and child anxiety and OCD therapist Natasha Daniels.

10. Healthy Women

Healthy Women is an online space that is all about women's health, wellness, and care. It centers on special health and mental health issues and how women have to deal with them, like endometriosis, menstrual disorders, anxiety, and more.

Healthy Women features a special category that focuses specifically on self-care and mental health. This section can help with anything from body image and sleep habits and disorders to brain health and relationships.

Practicing Good Mental Health and Well-Being as a Woman

Maintaining your mental health and well-being is vital. Forgetting about yourself could possibly lead to issues in your relationships, career, and physical health.

If you need a reminder to always look after your mental health and wellness, be sure to try these websites.
