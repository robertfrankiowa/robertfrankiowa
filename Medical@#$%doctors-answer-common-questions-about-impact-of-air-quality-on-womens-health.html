How does air pollution affect women's reproductive health and pregnancy outcomes?

Expert opinion from Dr. Mayank Saxena
Doctor of Medicine (MBBS) · 1 years of experience · India

Air pollution can also affect women's reproductive health as pregnant women is in immunosuppresive state and can catch infections easily. Also, exposure to the harmful gases present in the air can interfere with the normal development of the baby.

Expert opinion from Dr. Anet Varghese
Doctor of Medicine (MBBS) · 1 years of experience · India

Air pollution can negatively affect pregnancy, possibly leading to preterm birth, a low birth weight, stillbirth, or congenital abnormalities. People who live in polluted areas or have exposure to indoor air pollution from toxins such as cigarette smoke have higher rates of negative pregnancy outcomes.

What are the best ways to protect yourself and your baby from the harmful effects of air pollution during pregnancy and breastfeeding?

Expert opinion from Dr. Anet Varghese
Doctor of Medicine (MBBS) · 1 years of experience · India

Check the Air Quality Index (AQI) in your area. The AQI provides details about air quality and actions you can take to protect your health. Examples may include: limiting outdoor activity, using AC or having asthma inhalers ready. Know the risks of heat exposure in the workplace and learn how to avoid exposure to extreme heat and air pollution during pregnancy Keep babies cool and hydrated Never leave your children alone in a vehicle Electrify your home (electric stove, heat pump). Select power companies that use non-polluting energy, if possible and available in your area Choose higher rated filters for your home's heating, ventilation and air conditioning system. Look for a filter with a minimum efficiency reporting value rating of 13 or higher to catch smaller particles in the air. Stay up to date on mask guidance when the air is heavily polluted Consider zero-emission vehicles and use public transportation or walk and bike​ when it is safe.

How can poor air quality worsen or trigger symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes, mood swings, and insomnia?

Expert opinion from Dr. Himanshu J. Vats
Doctor of Medicine (MBBS) · 1.5 years of experience · India

Here are some ways how poor air quality can worsen or trigger symptoms of menopause: Air pollution can increase inflammation. Inflammation is a common symptom of menopause, and it can be worsened by air pollution. This is because air pollution can damage cells and tissues, which can lead to inflammation. Inflammation can also worsen hot flashes and other menopausal symptoms. Air pollution can disrupt sleep. Poor sleep is a common symptom of menopause, and it can be worsened by air pollution. This is because air pollution can irritate the airways and make it difficult to breathe, which can make it harder to fall asleep and stay asleep. Sleep deprivation can also worsen hot flashes and other menopausal symptoms. Air pollution can increase stress. Stress is a common trigger for hot flashes and other menopausal symptoms. Air pollution can increase stress levels by making it difficult to breathe and by disrupting sleep. Stress can also worsen hot flashes and other menopausal symptoms.
