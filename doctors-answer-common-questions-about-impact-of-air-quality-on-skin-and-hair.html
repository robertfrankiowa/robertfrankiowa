Doctors Answer Common Questions About Impact Of Air Quality On Skin And Hair.

How does air pollution affect skin and hair health and appearance?

Expert opinion from Dr. Anet Varghese
Doctor of Medicine (MBBS) · 1 years of experience · India

Pollution breaks down collagen and oxidizes the lipid layer in the skin, which impairs skin-barrier function, resulting in dehydration, sensitivity, uneven skin tone, dullness, acne, and premature aging. Also, these particles can bind to the hair's surface, infiltrating the follicle and settling on the surface of the scalp. Severe air pollution can alter the condition of the cuticle, giving hair a rough, brittle and dull appearance.

Skin comes in direct contact with the pollutants and particulate matter present in the polluted air and can cause its adverse effects like allergies and inflammation. It can also cause hair to fall as it can make the hair rough and thin.

What are the signs and symptoms of air pollution damage on skin and hair?

Expert opinion from Dr. Mayank Saxena
Doctor of Medicine (MBBS) · 1 years of experience · India

Polluted air can affect skin and hair, as they contain pollutants like particulate matter and harmful gases, which can damage the skin and hair, producing lesions on the skin and causing hair to fall when it comes into contact with the skin and hair.

Expert opinion from Dr. Anet Varghese
Doctor of Medicine (MBBS) · 1 years of experience · India

Air pollution is being shown to play an increasing causation role in our most common skin diseases. Repeated and frequent exposure to high levels of these pollutants may have deleterious effects on the skin, which include association with or causation of premature aging, photodamage, solar lentigines, melasma, and increased incidences of atopic dermatitis, psoriasis, skin cancers, hyperpigmentation and acne. Air pollution can significantly affect the health of your scalp and hair follicles. Studies state that hair loss due to air pollution can be similar to that caused by male-pattern baldness. It can also trigger other issues like scar alopecia, alopecia areata, and chronic telogen effluvium.

How can I protect my skin and hair from air pollution exposure and reduce its harmful effects?

Expert opinion from Dr. Mayank Saxena
Doctor of Medicine (MBBS) · 1 years of experience · India

Pollutants in the air can interact with exposed skin and hair and can cause adverse effects like skin irritation or allergy and hair damage. To protect the same using hair oil and skin cream is recommended. Apart from that, use of masks is also recommended to prevent respiratory effects that can be produced due to air pollution.
