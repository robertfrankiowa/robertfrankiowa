13 Things Experts Say Happen When You Eat Avocado Everyday

Are you craving that seemingly elusive avo-glow? We have great news — it’s not as hard to achieve as you might think.

There’s a reason avocados take over brunch menus, Instagram feeds, and the entire state of California. They pack a punch of health benefits that are hard to ignore. Nutrition experts consistently find new reasons to love this creamy, green fruit.

We reviewed extensive health studies to curate our list and examined how longevity experts weighed in. We sifted through all this information to find the science-backed benefits of eating avocados daily. Every item on our list is vetted, and we’re fairly confident avocados are the real superhero of the produce aisle.

Your Heart Gets Healthier

Avocados are superstars when it comes to your heart health. The mushy green goodness inside that rough exterior is high in monounsaturated fats, particularly oleic acid.

Clinical studies reveal that folks who incorporate avocados into their daily diet have lower LDL cholesterol (bad) and higher HDL cholesterol (good) levels. 

This dual action is crucial for maintaining a healthy heart. Reducing inflammation and improving blood flow lower the risk of heart disease. This magical balance means you’re taking steps to reduce plaque buildup in your arteries — the very ingredient that leads to heart attacks or strokes. It’s like giving your heart a protective shield.

Your Body Has Better Blood Pressure Control

Avocados could be vying for best friend status if high blood pressure has you routinely running to the cardiologist. These fruits (yes, they are fruits) are packed with potassium, a mineral that balances the effects of sodium to regulate blood pressure. Potassium works by relaxing the walls of your blood vessels, thereby lowering blood pressure.

Aside from your banana-obsessed buddy, most folks don’t get enough potassium in their diets. Just one avocado provides about 975 milligrams of potassium, roughly 21% of your recommended daily intake. Combine this with other heart-healthy fats, and you’ve joined forces with a powerful ally in maintaining cardiovascular health.

You Increase Anti-Inflammatory Properties

Avocados are more than just the connection between you and a bowl of guacamole. They’re also loaded with compounds like polyphenols and flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory effects. Inflammation is your body’s natural response to injury or illness, but chronic inflammation can cause heart disease, arthritis, and even cancer.

The polyphenols and flavonoids in avocados neutralize the harmful free radicals in your body. So, whether you’re dealing with chronic joint pain or want to stay ahead of potential health issues, avocados offer a delicious way to keep inflammation in check.

Your Eye Health Improves

Step aside carrots. Avocados could be the real secret to keeping your vision sharp. They’re a great source of lutein and zeaxanthin, two incredibly beneficial antioxidants for healthy eyes. They protect your eyes from harmful light waves and reduce the potential to develop cataracts or macular degeneration.

Lutein and zeaxanthin are found in high concentrations in the retina, where they help filter out damaging light and protect eye tissues from oxidative stress. Eating avocados regularly can help maintain these protective levels, ensuring your eyes stay healthy as you age.

You’ll Develop a More Regulated Digestive System

If you’ve ever struggled with digestive issues, you’ll be happy to hear that avocados can help. The secret is their fiber content, which aids in digestion and helps keep your bowel movements regular.

One avocado contains about 13 grams of fiber, or almost 50 percent of an adult’s recommended daily intake. This fiber content feeds your intestines with beneficial bacteria to promote a healthy gut — your microbiome plays a big part in your overall health, affecting everything from your immune system to your mood.

You Can Manage Your Weight More Effectively

Are you ready to call a truce in the daily battle with your belt loops? Try eating an avocado every day. The avocado’s healthy fats and fiber make you feel fuller for longer, potentially reducing overall calorie intake by curbing hunger. Feeling satisfied makes you less likely to reach for that bag of Oreos.

Studies show that people who eat avocados generally have a lower body mass index (BMI) and are less likely to gain weight over time. The monounsaturated fats in avocados boost metabolism and increase the rate at which you burn calories. 

You Improve Blood Sugar Regulation

Keeping blood sugar levels in check is crucial for overall health, especially if you’re dealing with diabetes or insulin resistance. An avocado’s healthy fats in avocados can help stabilize blood sugar levels by reducing blood sugar spikes and slowing digestion after meals.

Avocados have a low glycemic index, meaning you won’t experience a spike in blood sugar levels after eating one. This makes them an excellent choice for folks looking to manage their blood sugar naturally. The fiber content helps here, too, so the combination of healthy fats and fiber gives you that one-two punch for maintaining stable blood sugar levels.

You’ll Likely Notice Healthier Skin and Hair

Maybe it is actually Maybelline — or maybe it’s a daily dose of avocados. These little green beautifiers are packed with vitamins and fats that are beneficial for maintaining healthy skin and hair. The monounsaturated fats in avocados help provide your skin with a natural moisture barrier, keeping it hydrated and supple.

Avocados are full of vitamins C and E, which are essential for skin repair and maintenance. Vitamin E works to protect your skin from oxidative damage, while vitamin C supports collagen production, keeping your skin firm and youthful.

You Might Help Reduce Your Cancer Risk

While more research is needed, preliminary studies suggest that the nutrients and compounds in avocados may help reduce the risk of certain types of cancer for men, such as prostate and skin cancer. Avocados’ antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties are significant in this potential benefit.

The compounds in avocados can help mitigate cancer cell growth and start programming cell death in cancerous cells. This action helps prevent the spread of cancer and supports overall health.

You Increase Bone Health

Strong bones are vital for overall health, and avocados can help you maintain them. They contain vitamin K, which is essential for absorbing calcium and minerals. Without enough vitamin K, your body can’t effectively use the calcium you consume, weakening bones.

Adults typically need about 120 micrograms of daily vitamin K; just one avocado provides about 25% of that recommended intake. Vitamin K works alongside calcium and vitamin D to keep bones strong and healthy; maintaining bone health is crucial whether you’re young or old. Avocados offer an easy and delicious way to support this need.

You’ll Absorb Nutrients Better Across the Board

Did you know that eating avocados can help you absorb more nutrients from other foods? The healthy fats in avocados increase your ability to absorb fat-soluble nutrients such as vitamins A, D, E, and K from other sources. 

So, by adding avocados to your meals, you’re essentially leveling up your diet’s overall nutritional value. For example, adding avocado slices to a salad can help you absorb more vitamins and antioxidants from its vegetable counterparts.

You’ll Experience Noticeable Mental Health Improvements

It’s not all about the physical benefits of avocados. Their healthy fats, specifically omega-3 fatty acids, are crucial to brain function. Consuming avocados can enhance cognitive abilities, including memory and problem-solving skills. You can achieve sharper focus and better mental clarity by simply enjoying your favorite guacamole or avocado toast.

Another amazing benefit of omega-3 fatty acids is their impact on depression and anxiety. Half an avocado also contains around 82 micrograms of Folate, which is linked to reducing depression symptoms.

You Might Slow down Your Aging Appearance

Avocados are loaded with antioxidants that fight against unstable molecules (otherwise known as free radicals) that can damage cells and accelerate aging. Eating avocados regularly helps neutralize these harmful molecules, protecting your skin from premature aging.

One of the standout antioxidants in avocados is Vitamin E, which is quite famous for its skin-nourishing properties. It helps maintain your skin’s hydration and elasticity, giving you that youthful, glowing complexion. All the other nutrients and healthy fats also help your body function better and last longer. Is it the fountain of youth? Well — it might be the next best thing.
