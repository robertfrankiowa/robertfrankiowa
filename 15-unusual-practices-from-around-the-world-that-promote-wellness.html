15 Unusual Practices from Around the World That Promote Wellness

In our increasingly connected world, we have the opportunity to learn from and be inspired by the diverse health and wellness practices of cultures around the globe. From ancient traditions to modern innovations, these unique approaches to well-being offer a fresh perspective on how we can nourish our bodies, minds, and spirits. Let’s explore 15 unusual practices from around the world that promote wellness in fascinating and sometimes surprising ways.

Forest Bathing (Japan)

In Japan, the practice of shinrin-yoku, or “forest bathing,” involves immersing oneself in nature and engaging the senses to promote relaxation and reduce stress. Studies have shown that spending time in forests can lower cortisol levels, boost immune function, and even improve sleep quality. Forest bathing has gained popularity around the world as a simple and effective way to connect with nature and promote well-being.

Ayurveda (India)

Ayurveda is a traditional system of medicine that originated in India over 5,000 years ago. It emphasizes the importance of balancing the body’s three doshas (vata, pitta, and kapha) through diet, lifestyle, and herbal remedies. Ayurvedic practices like oil pulling, tongue scraping, and self-massage are believed to promote detoxification, improve digestion, and enhance overall health and vitality.

Fika (Sweden)

In Sweden, fika is a daily ritual that involves taking a break from work or other activities to enjoy coffee, pastries, and social interaction. This practice is believed to promote a sense of connection, reduce stress, and improve overall well-being. Fika is not just about the food and drink, but also about taking a moment to slow down, savor the present, and connect with others.

Tai Chi (China)

Tai chi is a traditional Chinese practice that involves slow, fluid movements and deep breathing. It is often described as “meditation in motion” and is believed to promote balance, flexibility, and mental clarity. Studies have shown that regular tai chi practice can improve balance and reduce the risk of falls in older adults, as well as reduce stress and improve overall quality of life.

Sauna (Finland)

In Finland, saunas are a beloved national tradition that involves spending time in a hot, dry room to promote relaxation, detoxification, and social bonding. Saunas are believed to improve circulation, boost immune function, and even reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease. Many Finns enjoy sauna sessions several times a week, often followed by a cold plunge or roll in the snow to further invigorate the body.

Siesta (Spain)

In Spain and other Mediterranean countries, the siesta is a traditional afternoon rest or nap that is believed to promote productivity, reduce stress, and improve overall well-being. Studies have shown that short naps can improve cognitive function, boost creativity, and even lower the risk of heart disease. While the traditional siesta is not as common as it once was, many people around the world are rediscovering the benefits of a midday rest.

Laughter Yoga (India)

Laughter yoga is a practice that originated in India and involves prolonged voluntary laughter as a form of exercise and stress relief. Laughter has been shown to boost endorphins, reduce cortisol levels, and even improve immune function. Laughter yoga clubs and classes have sprung up around the world as a fun and accessible way to promote well-being and social connection.

Kava (Pacific Islands)

Kava is a traditional beverage made from the root of the kava plant that is consumed in many Pacific Island cultures for its calming and euphoric effects. Kava is often used in social and ceremonial settings to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety, and enhance social bonding. While kava has gained popularity around the world as a natural alternative to anti-anxiety medications, it can also have side effects and interact with other drugs, so it should be used with caution.

Hygge (Denmark)

Hygge is a Danish concept that roughly translates to a sense of coziness, contentment, and well-being. It involves creating a warm and inviting atmosphere, often with candles, soft lighting, and comfortable furnishings, and enjoying simple pleasures like good food, good company, and a sense of togetherness. Hygge has been linked to increased happiness and life satisfaction, and has gained popularity around the world as a way to promote well-being and connection.

Earthing (Global)

Earthing, also known as grounding, is the practice of connecting the body directly to the earth’s surface, either by walking barefoot or using special conductive mats or sheets. Proponents of earthing believe that it can reduce inflammation, improve sleep, and promote overall health by allowing the body to absorb the earth’s natural electrical charge. While more research is needed to confirm these benefits, many people find earthing to be a simple and enjoyable way to connect with nature and promote well-being.

Acupuncture (China)

Acupuncture is a traditional Chinese medicine practice that involves inserting thin needles into specific points on the body to promote healing and balance. It is believed to stimulate the body’s natural healing processes, reduce pain and inflammation, and promote overall well-being. While the exact mechanisms of acupuncture are still not fully understood, it has gained widespread acceptance as a complementary therapy for a variety of health conditions.

Smudging (Native American)

Smudging is a traditional Native American practice that involves burning sacred herbs like sage, cedar, or sweetgrass to purify and bless a space or person. The smoke from the herbs is believed to clear negative energy, promote relaxation, and enhance spiritual connection. Smudging has gained popularity around the world as a way to create a sense of ritual and intention in one’s environment.

Qigong (China)

Qigong is a traditional Chinese practice that involves gentle movements, breathing exercises, and meditation to cultivate and balance the body’s life force energy, or qi. It is believed to promote physical health, mental clarity, and spiritual well-being. Qigong has been shown to improve balance, reduce stress, and even boost immune function, and is often used as a complementary therapy for a variety of health conditions.

Sound Healing (Global)

Sound healing is a practice that uses vibrations from instruments like singing bowls, gongs, and tuning forks to promote relaxation, reduce stress, and stimulate healing in the body. It is believed that different frequencies can resonate with different parts of the body and mind, promoting balance and well-being. Sound healing has gained popularity around the world as a gentle and accessible way to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

Wabi-Sabi (Japan)

Wabi-sabi is a Japanese aesthetic concept that embraces the beauty of imperfection, impermanence, and simplicity. It involves finding appreciation and contentment in the natural cycle of growth, decay, and transformation, and accepting things as they are rather than striving for perfection. Wabi-sabi has been linked to increased mindfulness, resilience, and overall well-being, and has gained popularity around the world as a way to cultivate a more grounded and accepting approach to life.
