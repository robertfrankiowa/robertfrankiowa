Processed Foods Have Been Linked to Insomnia: Here Are the 15 Worst Culprits

Processed foods are convenient and often irresistible, but they have a hidden cost: your sleep. So, if sleepless nights are becoming your new normal, you might want to take a closer look at your diet. Studies show that a diet containing lots of processed foods might be the culprit keeping us awake at night.

Lack of sleep can lead to numerous health issues, including a weakened immune system, mood swings, and difficulty concentrating. Understanding the connection between our diet and sleep patterns is vital for improving overall health.

Processed foods are often packed with artificial additives, unhealthy fats, and excessive sugar.  These substances can interfere with your body’s natural sleep cycles, making it harder to get a restful night’s sleep. Similarly, high-fat meals can be hard to digest, causing discomfort and disrupting sleep.

Here is a list of 15 processed foods linked to insomnia. You may take a massive step towards better sleep and improved health by cutting these out of your diet. Dig deeper into these foods and learn why they are keeping you awake.

1. Fast Food Burgers

Although fast food burgers are a convenient choice for a quick meal, they can significantly impact sleep quality. These burgers often contain high levels of unhealthy fats, which can cause indigestion and make it difficult to fall asleep. Ingredients like buttery buns, fatty meat, and processed cheese exacerbate this issue.

Fast food burgers are also loaded with sodium, disrupting your body’s electrolyte balance. High sodium intake can lead to dehydration and elevated blood pressure, making it hard for your body to relax and achieve restful sleep.

So, the next time you’re craving a burger, consider making your own with lean meat and whole-grain buns for a healthier alternative.

2. Sugary Cereals

Sugary cereals may be tasty and fun, but they can wreak havoc on your metabolism and blood sugar levels. Consuming them daily for breakfast or as an evening snack means you’re ingesting a lot of refined carbs and sugar. This leads to a rapid spike in blood sugar levels, followed by a sharp drop. Such fluctuations can leave you awake, anxious, and restless, disrupting your sleep cycle.

Moreover, sugary cereals often lack the essential nutrients your body needs to stay strong. Choose cereals high in fiber and low in added sugar for a healthier option. This way, your blood sugar remains stable, and you’ll enjoy better sleep.

3. Packaged Cakes

Packaged cakes contain unhealthy fats, lots of sugar, and artificial additives. These products act similarly to sugary cereals, causing blood sugar fluctuations and indigestion, which interrupt the sleep cycle. The artificial additives and colors that make these foods more attractive are also linked to hyperactivity in children, which disrupts their sleep patterns.

These factors can negatively impact your and your family’s quality of sleep, so avoid them in the evening or before bedtime. Instead, choose healthy snacks such as fruits and nuts. 

4. Instant Noodles

Instant noodles are this generation’s favorite due to their low costs, ready-to-eat convenience, and the variety of flavors available. But they are rich in sodium and bad fats. Have you ever felt incredibly thirsty after having noodles? If yes, it’s because high sodium consumption can lead to dehydration, making sleeping difficult. 

For a healthier choice, try whole-grain noodles with lean protein and fresh vegetables. You’ll get more nourishment this way and avoid the drawbacks of instant noodles. 

5. Processed Cheese

Cheese spreads and processed slices are staples in many homes. However, these products frequently contain high levels of harmful fats and sodium, which can disrupt the sleep cycle. 

Furthermore, the additives in processed cheese can upset your body’s natural rhythm and exacerbate insomnia. For a healthier alternative, choose natural cheeses that are free of artificial additives and have a reduced salt content. This way, you can eat cheese without compromising your sleep. 

6. Soda

Soda is incredibly popular, particularly among young people, but it is also one of the worst beverages for your sleep. Most sodas are laden with sugar and caffeine, which can significantly impair your ability to rest. The sugar causes dramatic spikes and drops in your blood sugar levels, leading to restless nights.

Even caffeine-free sodas often contain artificial ingredients. Instead of reaching for a soda, consider water or herbal tea—healthier choices.

7. Pizza

Frozen pizza offers quick and convenient meals, perfect for immediate needs or unexpected guests. However, have you considered its impact on your body? Like other high-sugar and high-sodium foods, it can leave you dehydrated, cause digestive issues, and contribute to insomnia.

No one is suggesting you cut pizza out of your life forever. Instead, try making your own at home with natural, homemade ingredients. This way, you can enjoy pizza while controlling what you’re eating and maintaining a healthier lifestyle.

8. Candy

Who doesn’t love candy? It’s the perfect snack to satisfy a sweet tooth. However, it’s important to remember that our favorite treats are often packed with unhealthy sugars and artificial additives, which can wreak havoc on blood sugar levels.

Instead of ignoring your sweet cravings, choose alternatives like dark chocolate or fresh fruits. These options can satisfy your desire for something sweet without compromising your health.

9. Potato Chips

Eating potato chips before bed can leave you jittery and thirsty, keeping your mind and body awake. It can even lead to nightmares and cause indigestion and frequent trips to the bathroom during the night.

Instead of compromising your sleep quality for greasy chips full of bad fats, opt for some air-popped popcorn as a healthier alternative.

10. Energy Drinks

You may have seen ads claiming that energy drinks can instantaneously boost a person’s energy levels, causing them to emit electric currents or even sprout wings to fly. These depictions are pure fantasy. When consumed frequently, energy drinks can harm your health, particularly your sleep and kidneys. While these drinks might offer a quick energy boost, they can seriously disrupt sleep patterns.

The high levels of caffeine and sugar in these beverages can keep you awake all night. Caffeine lingers in your system for about five hours, leaving you counting sheep instead of getting the rest you need. The rollover effect on your next day sees you tired and lethargic.

11. Ice Cream

Nobody can say no to ice cream. But this sweet treat contains unhealthy amounts of sugar and bad fats. So next time you head to the store for ice cream, ensure it’s not in the evening or anywhere near your bedtime unless you want to be tossing and turning all night. 

If you still crave a sweet treat at night, try some yogurt and fruit or make an iced fruit smoothie instead to satisfy your ice creamy cravings with natural, healthy sweetness loaded with nutrients. 

12. Processed Meats

All the go-to, processed, and ready-to-eat meats are red flags for your health. Nothing comes without unhealthy fats and high sodium levels, be it bacon, sausage, or deli meats. These are not only linked to insomnia but also chronic high blood pressure.

Get yourself fresh meat and prepare it at home with natural ingredients. Ensure you eat at least three hours before bedtime so the dinner is fully digested before you sleep. 

13. Doughnuts

Doughnuts are a popular breakfast item, usually enjoyed with coffee. However, they are overloaded with trans fats and refined sugar. A person who eats a lot of donuts experiences constant blood sugar crashes, leading to restlessness and agitation at night.

Consider healthier breakfast options that satisfy your sweet desire instead of grabbing a doughnut. Almond butter on whole-grain toast or fresh fruit can offer a natural sweetness without interfering with your sleep. These substitutes also contain a lot of fiber, which lowers blood sugar and helps deeper, more restful sleep.

14. Fried Foods

Fried chicken, french fries, onion rings, and other fried foods are comfort foods for many people. However, due to the unhealthy ingredients added to them and the unhealthy trans fats they are cooked in, these are the most significant food items linked to insomnia. 

Go for grilled or baked foods like baked sweet potato fries and grilled chicken breasts to have a healthier yet equally tasty meal that doesn’t leave you lethargic but unable to rest well at night.

15. Canned Soup

Canned soups are a super convenient option in a rush but can have drawbacks—the amount of sodium and preservatives can mess with your sleep. So, while canned soups are quick and easy, it’s worth checking the labels and looking for some lower-sodium, more natural options if you want a good night’s sleep.

Homemade soup is a great way to get a good night’s sleep. Use fresh veggies, lean protein, and whole grains to make a tasty and nutritious soup.
