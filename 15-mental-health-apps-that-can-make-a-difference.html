15 Mental Health Apps That Can Make a Difference

Looking for mental and emotional support at your fingertips? While finding therapy might seem daunting, you can use several mental health support apps instead.

Managing stress and anxiety can be overwhelming to deal with on your own. Mental health apps can be an emotional toolkit to support you through mindfulness exercises, guided meditation sessions, and access to a supportive community.

Mental health apps can help you manage stress, sleep better, beat bad habits, and live healthier lives. These apps offer various ways to improve your mental health, from self-guided CBT exercises to virtual sessions with licensed therapists.

1. Headspace

You don’t have to deal with stress, anxiety, and insomnia alone anymore. The Headspace app offers guided meditation sessions, sleep soundscapes, mini-meditation exercises, and stress management courses to help you manage your mental health. Personalized recommendations ensure you are getting exactly the help you need. You can also track your progress through the app to effectively meet your mental health goals.

2. Calm

Anxiety can disrupt your sleep cycle, leading to fatigue, irritability, and stress, which makes life more difficult. The Calm app offers guided meditation and breathing exercises to help you manage your anxiety. It also provides soothing music, sleeping aids, nature scenes, and soothing sounds to help you relax before sleeping. You can also track your sleep schedule and take classes with mindfulness experts to understand your condition.

3. Talkspace

If you don’t have the time to attend a therapy session, Talkspace can bring one to you. With this app, you can text, chat, call, or video chat with licensed mental health professionals at your convenience. Flexible scheduling allows you to schedule a therapy session at any time, and you can message your therapist whenever you feel overwhelmed. The app offers affordable monthly subscription plans, so mental health support is available to everyone who needs it.

4. MoodKit

Journaling is a great way to release stress, understand your triggers, and lead a healthier life. MoodKit features a mood journal and other stress management tools to help you track your emotions and learn coping skills. Journaling prompts ensure you regularly update the journal, while relaxation exercises and customizable features help you manage stress and anxiety. The app also offers educational resources so you can stay informed.

5. Recovery Record

If you suffer from eating disorders, including anorexia, bulimia, orthorexia, and more, Recovery Record can help you cope with your condition. The app offers meal tracking, mood logging, and coping skills exercises. Customizable goals allow you to manage your recovery at your own pace. Users also get access to a supportive community of peers and professionals, making their recovery journey more manageable.

6. Sanvello

Peer support can do wonders for your mental health. Sanvello works on this principle and offers users access to safe and supportive online community forums. The app also features mood tracking, guided meditations, and cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) exercises. Look into this app if holistic mental health support is something that interests you.

7. Daylio

Daily mood journaling can help you identify behavior patterns and areas for improvement. With Daylio, you can track your mood and log your activities to understand your triggers and how to manage them. The app also offers gratitude journaling if you’re interested in doing some self-reflection. You can track your progress and see how far you’ve come.

8. Woebot

Woebot is an AI-powered chatbot perfect for individuals who want a safe space to discuss their feelings without judgment. The chatbot offers responses based on CBT techniques so you can reach out whenever you want to vent about your day. Talking to the AI will help you articulate your feelings and understand what makes you tick.

9. I Am Sober

Quitting alcohol is a harrowing task that requires self-control and resilience. I Am Sober helps individuals on their journey to sobriety by encouraging them to identify their triggers and develop healthy habits. The app offers features like daily pledges, a days sober tracker, motivational messages, and access to a supportive community.

10. Mindshift CBT

This evidence-based app helps users manage their anxiety and stress through cognitive behavioral therapy tools and techniques. Features like mood tracking, personalized goal setting, and guided meditations allow users to get their anxiety and phobias under control. The app also offers psychoeducation and a peer support community for inspiration and encouragement.

11. iBreathe

Stress-induced panic attacks can be kept at bay by practicing simple breathing exercises to calm yourself down. iBreathe offers a collection of deep-breathing exercises that are simple enough for anyone to follow. These exercises can help users manage their stress and anxiety in the moment. The app integrates with Apple Health.

12. Happify

Happify offers science-based activities and games that help users manage stress and boost their mood. Mood tracking, gratitude exercises, and happiness assessments are just some of the features the app offers. Happify is great for users interested in positive psychology and mental well-being.

13. Quit That

Do you wish you could quit a bad habit? Quit That provides tools and support to individuals wanting to quit bad habits and addictions. Progress charts, milestone celebrations, and accountability tools keep users on track, while motivational reminders keep their spirits up.

14. NOCD

NOCD uses exposure and response prevention (ERP) exercises to help people who have obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD). Subscribers are matched with a licensed therapist who provides personalized support through video sessions. You can also message your therapist in between sessions for additional support whenever needed.

15. Mind Moose

Mind Moose is a great app to teach kids about mental health and how to manage their emotions. The app helps children understand how their brains work through evidence-based modules called “Moose Missions” that earn kids badges and certificates when they complete them. The app aims to foster healthy self-esteem and body image and help children find inner strength.
