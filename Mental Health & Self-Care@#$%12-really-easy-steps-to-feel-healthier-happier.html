12 Really Easy Steps To Feel Healthier & Happier

Achieving health and happiness might appear challenging, but implementing daily habits and changes can boost your physical, mental, and emotional well-being, especially if you stay dedicated and committed. Let’s look at twelve easy strategies for being healthy and happy that can fit any lifestyle.

Drink Water When You Wake Up

Staying hydrated is important to staying healthy. Starting your day with a glass of water can rev up your metabolism, cleanse your system of toxins, and replenish hydration levels after sleeping. For an extra wellness kick, infuse your water with a slice of lemon to enhance detoxification and get a splash of vitamin C.

Take a moment to breathe

Spending a few minutes of your day participating in mindful breathing can significantly lower stress levels and enhance mental sharpness. Practice easy breathwork by taking a deep breath in through your nose, pausing momentarily, and then exhaling through the mouth. You can do this practically anytime throughout the day.

Make sure you eat in the morning

Kicking off your day with a well-rounded, nutritious breakfast can really shape your entire day. You want a balanced mix of healthy fat, carbs, and protein to energize both your body and mind. Try starting with a hearty bowl of oatmeal with different fruits and nuts on top or a healthy smoothie packed with whole-food goodness.

regularly move your body

Sticking to a regular physical activity routine can help you stay healthy and happy. Most days, aim for 30 minutes or more of daily exercise or physical activity. It could be a vigorous walk, a relaxing yoga session, a lively dance class, or a bike ride, but choose an activity that brings you joy to ensure it becomes a long-lasting practice.

Spend Time Outside

Spending time outdoors can greatly enhance your well-being. Immersing yourself in nature and spending time outdoors reduces stress levels and boosts your mood and mental health. Whether it’s time by the lake, hiking a trail, or sitting outside in the sun, make it a point to spend time outdoors.

be grateful for the little things

Developing a gratitude practice can transform your perspective from what’s missing to the abundance you possess. Jot down five things you’re thankful for daily, right then and there. Making this a habit can nurture a more joyful, optimistic view of life and prompt you to appreciate the blessings already in your surroundings.

Limit Screen Time

Too much time spent staring at a screen can strain your eyes and lead to bad sleep and higher stress levels. Avoid electronics in the evening when you’re getting ready for bed. Instead, spend that time winding down with activities that don’t involve screens, like reading, doing your nighttime skincare routine, or meditating.

Eat More Natural Food

Whole foods, such as fruits, vegetables, grains, and protein, provide your body with nutrients that improve your overall well-being and provide healthy energy that helps you feel better. Try not to overindulge in food with processed ingredients, sugar, or salt. Incorporate more colorful, healthy, and nourishing foods into your diet for improved health and happiness.

Make sure you get enough sleep

Having a healthy and consistent sleep schedule and getting enough rest is imperative for physical and mental health. You should be getting between 7 and 9 hours of sleep each night. If you struggle with this, try to develop a relaxing bedtime routine for yourself. Don’t drink caffeine, and try to avoid electronics close to bedtime.

Embrace human connection

Having people around you that you can count on, trust, and connect with can improve your mood, reduce your anxiety, and make you feel less lonely in the world. Carve time out of your life to cultivate connections with friends and family. When you tend to and care for meaningful connections in your life, they can last you a lifetime and improve your well-being and quality of life for many years to come.

Pour into your own cup

Self-care is an important part of pouring into your own cup so that you can pour into those you love and allow happiness into your life. Prioritize doing things that bring you happiness and make you feel calm, relaxed, and at peace. Make self-care a regular and consistent part of your routine without the shame and guilt of focusing on yourself.

Practice Meditation Or Mindfulness Regularly

Meditation is an incredibly useful tool for reducing stress and feeling happier and healthier daily. Even a few minutes of meditation daily can help calm your mind and strengthen your emotional resilience. There are so many meditation techniques to explore, from guided meditations to mindfulness practices, that involve nothing more than taking a few minutes to be grateful.
